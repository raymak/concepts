Frequently asked questions

What is it?

What exactly is Firefox Better Web?

Firefox Better Web is an initiative to bring back the joy of browsing the web. It combines Scroll and Firefox Enhanced Tracking Protection:


1) Scroll collaborates with partner sites in its network to create ad-free versions of their content. It also offers audio for articles within the partner network, and measures your reading activity across devices and apps so that you can see how your reading activity directly benefits the partner sites you patronize. Scroll pays its partner sites based on reading activity. These payments amount to over 40% more than what partners would have received through ad revenue.


2) Firefox Enhanced Tracking Protection (ETP) technology is set to block trackers, cryptominers, and fingerprinters, giving you increased performance while being protected online. The Firefox Better Web add-on works only on the Firefox desktop browser.


Firefox Better Web supports the possibility of an internet that’s not dominated by ads and ad-driven revenue. It paves the way toward quality online content and a fast, clean, and private online experience—for all.

What is a tracker? Why should it matter to me that Firefox Better Web is blocking them?

A tracker is something that companies place into websites that can gather very detailed information about you, often without you realizing it. That information could include your exact location at the moment, your financial situation, your health concerns, and much more.


Some companies sell the extensive data that trackers collect about you to others that might want to influence your behavior. For example, they might want you to purchase their products, influence your political beliefs, or manipulate you into changing your behavior.


This mass collection of personal information on the internet greatly increases the risk that your data will be misused or leaked, and that organizations you have never even heard of will know startlingly intimate details about you and your family.


Firefox Better Web uses Firefox’s built in Enhanced Tracking Protection (ETP) technology to automatically limit the collection of your personal data without your consent. It blocks a wide range of trackers, from common “everyday” trackers to those using more sophisticated data mining techniques. Read more about online tracking and how Firefox can help you.

What do you mean that this is in beta?

The Firefox Better Web initiative is still in beta, which means that the product is not fully developed and scaled. During this stage, we intend to learn more about whether and how to develop Firefox Better Web into a full-fledged product.


How it works

Does it work on all websites? What about my apps and devices?

Firefox Better Web works on all websites when you’re on the Firefox desktop browser. The Scroll-specific features—syncing, audio, and reading lists, as well as publisher direct payout—work only on partner publisher sites. To use Scroll features on all your browsers, devices and apps, use the set-up links or download the Scroll app, depending on where you read. Learn more about Scroll set-up.

Will Firefox Better Web work with my current ad-blocker?

Yes, Firefox Better Web will work with your current ad-blocker. For some ad-blockers, you may need to adjust settings to ensure it is not interfering with Scroll.

Privacy

Does Scroll work with my browser’s privacy settings?

Scroll does work with a browser’s privacy settings, but for some browsers you may need to manually allow “third-party cookies” from Scroll. Cookies from Scroll are used to give you the ad-free experience on partner sites and to allocate your money to the sites you visit. Data is only collected when you visit partner sites while logged in to Scroll.

Does Firefox or Scroll sell my data?

No, neither Firefox nor Scroll sell your data.


Scroll's Privacy Policy protects you when you are on Scroll partner sites. On any other sites you visit while using Firefox, you are protected by the Firefox Personal Data Promise. Scroll measures your engaged time spent on partner sites to calculate how much they should earn each month. You can manage the data collected by Scroll on scroll.com and learn about how Scroll looks after your personal data in the Scroll Privacy Policy.
